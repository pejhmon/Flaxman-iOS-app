` In this composition Dürer explores the supreme ability of the engraved line to capture the natural surface effects of essentially linear details, such as fur and grasses.
The marvellously crisp quality of line in this print indicates that it is an impression taken from the plate soon after it was engraved, before it became worn through use. This print would have been purchased by relatively wealthy individuals. Unlike many of the far cheaper popular woodcuts that would have been available at this time, this image would not have been displayed within the home but kept alongside others like it, within the leaves of an album or prayer book.
